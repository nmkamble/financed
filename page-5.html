Basics of Credit/Credit Score -

  • CREDIT: Credit is known as your borrowing reputation or the amount of money you are approved to borrow.
  • INSTALLMENT CREDIT: The debt you repay on a fixed schedule, usually the loans will have an interest rate. Examples inlcude: mortages, car loans, and credit loans.
  • REVOLVING: This type of credit lets you borrow money up to a certain amount, and is paid off monthly. Examples include credit cards: Visa, Mastercard, Gas cards, etc.
  • SERVICE CREDIT: This type of credit is where the borrower pays for a service at a later time. One example is a membership.

Common Credit Terms:

Annual Percentage Rate: This is the interest rate that you will pay each YEAR from the loan balance. So if you borrowed $2,000 and your APR is 10% then you will pay $200 every year per $2,000 borrowed.

Annual Fee: You may be charged with this fee by your credit card company for the use of a credit card each year.These fees are common and usually different than Interest or other rate fees.

Grace Period: This is the time frame after your bill due date, where you are allowed to pay your bill without interest rate or late fees


How to Keep a Good Credit Score:

  • Pay your bills on time, set schedules, and budget. You can check out our budgeting page to learn more on how to budget.
  • Monitor your credit cards, make sure there is no theft, or suspicious activity. Monitoring it weekly is essential.
  • Have a plan to pay back your loans. If you are a young adult you might have plans to pay back a car loan or etc. Make sure you make a plan and follow it.
  • Keep your credit card balances low.
  • Keep emergency funds, and do not exceed your credit card limit.
