Types of Loans:

  • SECURED: The lender can seize something if the loan is not paid back, lower interest rates because there is collateral.
  • UNSECURED: do not require any collateral from the borrower, higher interest rates because there is no protection.

Terms to Know:

PRINCIPAL: original amount of money borrowed

INTEREST RATE: the lenderâ€™s charge for the use of their money

  • FIXED RATES: fixed and unchanging
  • VARIABLE RATES: can change over time, based on a standard market rate

TERM: length of time that the borrower has to pay back the loan

  • Longer term = higher interest rate
  • Loans can be paid off before the term is up

Fees Associated with Loans:

  • APPLICATION FEE: for approving a loan
  • PROCESSING FEE: for administering a loan
  • ORIGINATION FEE: for securing a loan (common for mortgages)
  • ANNUAL FEE: yearly flat fee paid to the lender (common for credit cards)
  • LATE FEE: charges for late payments
  • PREPAYMENT FEE: for paying a loan off early (common for home and car loans)

Some Factors that Lenders Consider:

  • Age
  • Debt-to-income ratio
  • Credit score
  • Liquid assets
  • Employment history
